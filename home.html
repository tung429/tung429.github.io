Introduction of background Lack of chargers

“The inadequate of electric vehicle chargers can be an obstacle for the popularity of electric vehicles” said Stephen Cheung, a director in Schneider Electric (Hong Kong). He stated that the chargers mostly built in the commercial area and public parking, which has a distance from the dwellings. This situation reflected that those electric vehicles need to compete with gasoline cars for parking spaces. The parking spaces for electric vehicles may be used even the parking have the charging facility. Therefore, the government should expand the area of parking space which support electric vehicle chargers.

To solve the problem, the Government announced on the latest Budget that they allocated 120 million dollars to expand the charging network for electric vehicles in the public parking. There are in total 11,548 registered electric vehicles in Hong Kong until January 2019. However, there is a slow growth on the number of electric vehicles, mainly due to the end of the plan “Resale Value Guarantee*” of Tesla last year. Therefore, it is planned to increase 1000 public chargers to achieve the number to 1700 chargers before 2022.

  *The program guaranteed the resale value of a Tesla Model S after three years when purchased through one of the company’s loan financing programs.

Since there is no a single webpage in the market that gather that can gather information about all charging stations and not every charging station has the same configuration for example, a different adapter, quick charger, etc. , making it hard for users to search for charging stations they need. According to the figures provided by the Environmental Protection Agency, there were 1036 charging devices in Hong Kong in 2013, and there were only 314 licensed electric vehicles at the time; however, the number of electric cars in Hong Kong increased in 5 years until October 2017 The number of licensed electric vehicles has reached 10385, which is a 32-fold increase. Nowadays, there are a total of 1,846 charging devices in Hong Kong. Although the growth rate in the five years is 78%, it is far behind the number of electric vehicles. There is only one charging station per 5.6 cars.






Form the chart, most of the district cannot response to the demands. Tuen Mun, Tai Po and Southern district severely insufficient of charger. Kwai Tsing, Tsuen Wan, Wong Tai Sin, Kowloon City, Sai Kung, Sham Shui Po and Yuen Long are under 100 chargers, the number is not satisfied. 


Relatively, Kwun Tong has enough electric vehicle chargers, there are 337 chargers in Kwun Tong, over ten times that of the number of Tuen Mun.
